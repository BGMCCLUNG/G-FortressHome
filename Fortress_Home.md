## The Three Archetypes

Any property must match at least one archetype. See `/references/` folder for images.

### Reference 1 — The Brick Fortress
**London Georgian/Victorian Townhouse — All-Black Brick**

3-story narrow brick rowhouse. Matte black over everything — brick, trim, cornice, ironwork, window frames. Projecting bay window stack running full height. Mansard-influenced roofline with white balustrade parapet as the only contrast break. Arched ground-floor entry with transom. Wrought iron fence at street, below-grade basement access. Warm amber interior light bleeding through tall multi-pane windows. The darkness reads as depth, not absence — because the brick texture shows through.

- **Character:** Mass. Weight. Permanence. Ancient and immovable.
- **Hunt target:** Intact historic brick rowhouse, pre-1920, with original ornament.
- **Reference image:** `references/ref1_london_brick_fortress.jpg`

### Reference 2 — The Carved Fortress
**San Francisco Italianate Victorian — All-Black Wood-Frame**

2-story wood-frame Italianate. All-black unifies the entire structure into a single object. Extraordinary paneled cornice parapet with layered bracketing and dentil detail — reads as a crown. Double arched entry bay at ground level with pilaster columns. Integrated garage at street level with marble/granite stair rising alongside. Gold hardware as the only warm accent — restrained punctuation against the black. Key insight: the all-black treatment makes the material irrelevant. The ornament is what the eye reads.

- **Character:** Ornament. Detail. Jewel-box precision. A singular object, not a house.
- **Hunt target:** Intact wood-frame Italianate with restorable cornice and bracket detail.
- **Reference image:** `references/ref2_sf_italianate_carved.jpg`

### Reference 3 — The Dark Insertion
**London Contemporary Dark Insertion — Stripped Brick Shell**

4-story dark brick shell — original Victorian masonry, charcoal-reading exterior, zero applied ornament. Contemporary intervention: oversized steel-framed picture windows running nearly full height, woven/chain metal screen panels layered over the glass that diffuse interior light into amber glow visible from street. Subtle black metal spires or crenellations at roofline — gothic punctuation that rewards patience. The drama is entirely interior-out.

- **Character:** Restraint. Contrast. Interior fire visible from the street.
- **Hunt target:** Neglected/gutted brick shell — cheapest acquisition, highest transformation potential.
- **Reference image:** `references/ref3_london_dark_insertion.jpg`

## Core Property Criteria

### Non-Negotiables

| Criteria | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Style** | Victorian gothic, Queen Anne, Italianate, Second Empire — brick OR wood-frame with intact ornament OR stripped brick shell (Dark Insertion). |
| **Garage / Parking** | ⚠️ NON-NEGOTIABLE — garage (attached, detached, integral, or alley) OR private driveway required. Street parking only = automatic disqualifier. No exceptions. |
| **Street Surface** | ⚠️ Paved only. Cobblestone or brick street surface = automatic disqualifier. |
| **Year Built** | Pre-1920 preferred / pre-1900 ideal. |
| **Condition** | Fixer-upper — structurally sound shell acceptable. Livable or made livable quickly. |
| **Bedrooms** | 3+ minimum. |
| **Stories** | 2–3 stories preferred. |
| **Acquisition Price** | Under $150,000 target / under $75,000 ideal. |
| **Status** | Tax delinquent, extended DOM, estate sale, bank-owned, vacant, or condemned (PLI score 1 only). |
| **Primary Zip Codes** | 15212 · 15233 · 15214 · 15215 |

### Automatic Disqualifiers

- [ ] No garage AND no private driveway — street parking only
- [ ] Cobblestone or brick street surface
- [ ] Structurally compromised foundation (actual failure, not cosmetic)
- [ ] Fire damage beyond cosmetic — char into framing, roof loss
- [ ] Price above $200K before negotiation
- [ ] PLI Inspection Score of 2, 3, or 4
- [ ] Wood-frame with stripped ornament — no cornice, no brackets, no pilasters
- [ ] HOA with restrictive aesthetic covenants conflicting with all-black transformation
- [ ] Modern construction post-1930

### Bonus Criteria (3+ = shortlist regardless of minor issues)

- [ ] Original woodwork / millwork intact
- [ ] Decorative fireplaces (working or cosmetic)
- [ ] Exposed brick interior
- [ ] Turret, bay window, or tower element
- [ ] Stained glass windows
- [ ] Covered porch / front stoop
- [ ] High ceilings (10ft+)
- [ ] Original hardwood floors
- [ ] Basement / cellar
- [ ] Garage is integral / street-level
- [ ] Corner lot or double lot
- [ ] Neighboring properties already rehabbed

## PLI Inspection Scoring — Critical Knowledge

Pittsburgh PLI (Permits, Licenses and Inspections) scores all viewable structural elements.

| PLI Score | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Structurally intact — no immediate observable danger. Condemned but physically sound. | ✅ Research further — viable candidate |
| **2** | Structurally compromised, unsafe, possibly dangerous. | ❌ Pass |
| **3** | Structurally compromised, unsafe, and dangerous. | ❌ Hard pass |
| **4** | Imminently dangerous. | ❌ Do not pursue |

**To check:** [pittsburghpa.agencycounter.com](https://pittsburghpa.agencycounter.com) or call PLI: (412) 255-2175

## Acquisition Strategy

Programs can be stacked. The URA grant alone covers most acquisition prices in the target range.

| Program | Details |
|---|---|
| **URA Pittsburgh** | Up to $90K incl. $50K upfront grant. Income ≤80% AMI. 10-year residency. **ANCHOR PROGRAM.** |
| **PHFA K-FIT** | Up to 5% of purchase price, forgiven over 10 years. No dollar cap. No lead paint restriction. |
| **PHFA K-DATE (2026)** | 8% of purchase price for loans ≤$150K. Zero interest, 30-year term, no monthly payments. |
| **PHFA HOMEstead** | Up to $10K forgivable DPA. NOTE: pre-1978 homes may not qualify due to lead paint regs. |
| **First Front Door** | 3:1 match up to $5K–$15K via FHLB Pittsburgh. Apply early — funds run out. |
| **Pittsburgh Land Bank** | Direct purchase, often $1–$5K, rehab commitment required. |
| **FHA 203(k) Rehab Loan** | Purchase + renovation in one loan. Ideal for livable fixer-uppers. |
| **Sheriff / Tax Claim Sale** | Judicial sale = free and clear of liens. Watch monthly. |
| **Conservatorship (Act 135)** | File on blighted property, rehab it, acquire title. 12–18 months. |
| **Direct Owner Contact** | Human letter to absentee/delinquent owners. Best off-market channel. |

## Exterior Transformation Sequences

### Sequence A — Brick / Wood-Frame Fortress (References 1 & 2)

| Phase | Work | Est. Materials Cost |
|---|---|---|
| 1 — Lighting | Warm amber bulbs 2200–2700K all street-visible floors. Do this first. | $200–$500 |
| 2 — Iron & Hardware | Paint all iron matte black. Replace chrome/brass with aged black iron. Warm brass accents only. | $500–$1,500 |
| 3 — Windows | Repaint frames matte black. Steel or black-painted wood sash only. | $2,000–$8,000 |
| 4 — Exterior Paint | Full matte black. SW Tricorn Black SW 6258 or Iron Ore SW 7069. One color, no contrast breaks. | $3,000–$8,000 |
| 5 — Roofline Crown | White balustrade or stone coping — sole contrast element. | $2,000–$6,000 |
| 6 — Street Layer | Dark plantings, iron gate, dark stone path. | $1,000–$3,000 |
| **TOTAL** | **Materials only — your labor is the equity** | **$8,700–$27,000** |

### Sequence B — Dark Insertion (Reference 3 — Stripped Brick Shell)

| Phase | Work |
|---|---|
| 1 — Structural Assessment | Engineer evaluates masonry for window enlargement feasibility before committing. |
| 2 — Window Openings | Oversized steel-framed picture windows. Black powder-coated steel only. |
| 3 — Screen Layer | Woven/chain metal mesh panels over glass. Source: Haver & Boecker, Cambridge Architectural, or custom. Creates amber glow. |
| 4 — Exterior Darkening | Masonry stain (not paint) — Cabot Masonry Stain or equivalent. Lets brick texture read through. |
| 5 — Roofline Spires | Custom black steel gothic crenellations at parapet. Subtle — rewards patience. |
| 6 — Entry & Street | Black steel door, amber sidelight, warm brass hardware, iron gate, dark stone threshold. |
